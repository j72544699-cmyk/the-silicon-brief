$65 Billion in One Week: What the Anthropic Pile-On Tells You About the AI Race
Google and Amazon both wrote massive checks to the same AI startup this week. That's not competition — that's a signal about where the real value in AI is consolidating.
$65Bcommitted to one AI startup in a single week
30×Anthropic revenue growth in 16 months
11.7×sales multiple at $350B — cheaper than Nvidia

This week Google committed up to $40 billion into Anthropic — $10 billion upfront, $30 billion more tied to performance benchmarks. Two days earlier Amazon announced a $5 billion investment with an option for up to $20 billion more. Both deals pegged Anthropic's valuation at $350 billion.

Here's what makes this unusual. Google and Amazon are direct competitors — both building their own frontier AI models. Yet both are pouring capital into a third-party AI lab simultaneously. That tells you something important: even the hyperscalers don't fully trust their own models to win. They're hedging at scale.

The Number That Changes Everything

Anthropic's annualized revenue went from $1 billion at end of 2024 to $9 billion at end of 2025 to $30 billion as of early April 2026. That's 30x growth in 16 months. At a $350 billion valuation that's under 12x sales — cheaper on a revenue multiple than Nvidia trades today. Google isn't being generous. It's getting a bargain.

What this means for your portfolio

The obvious read is Alphabet and Amazon as buys — both now have massive leveraged exposure to what may be the fastest-growing revenue story in tech history. But the more interesting signal is what this does to the rest of the AI stack.

When two hyperscalers commit this much compute and capital to one model provider, it creates a gravitational pull. Enterprises building on AI will increasingly default to the models backed by the infrastructure they already use. That's a moat being built in real time — and it narrows the field for everyone else.

Buy Signal

Alphabet (GOOGL) and Amazon (AMZN) — both now have performance-linked upside in Anthropic's growth at below-market multiples, plus compute revenue from the deal itself.

Caution

Mid-tier AI model companies with no hyperscaler backing. This week's deals make the rich richer. The moat between top-3 and everyone else just got wider.

Watch For

Anthropic IPO timing. The tender offer to employees at $350B signals they're prepping. If they IPO at $500B+ later this year, Google's entry looks genius in hindsight.

Red Flag

The $30B from Google is performance-contingent. If Anthropic misses benchmarks, the deal shrinks significantly. Don't model the full $65B as guaranteed.

The metric to track

Watch Anthropic's annualized revenue at each earnings cycle for Google and Amazon. Both companies will now report Anthropic-related metrics. If revenue growth holds above 20x year-over-year, the $350B valuation looks cheap and both stocks benefit. If growth decelerates, the $30B contingent tranche from Google may never land — and the narrative shifts fast.

The Bottom Line

$65 billion in a week isn't irrational exuberance — it's two of the most sophisticated capital allocators on the planet making a calculated bet that Anthropic is undervalued at $350 billion. The 30x revenue growth in 16 months gives them the data to back it up. For investors, the trade isn't Anthropic — it's the companies that just bought leveraged exposure to it at a discount.
